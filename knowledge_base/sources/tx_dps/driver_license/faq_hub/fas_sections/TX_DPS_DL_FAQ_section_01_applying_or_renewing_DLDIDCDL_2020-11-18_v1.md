# Texas DPS — FAQ Section 1: Applying for or Renewing a DL/ID/CDL

Source: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/faq/section-1-applying-or-renewing-dlidcdl  
Publisher: Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS)  
Page date: 2020-11-18  
Retrieved at: 2026-01-22  

---

## Q1. Do I have to go into a driver license office to obtain a Texas driver license for the first time?
Yes. First-time Texas applicants and people transferring from another state must visit a driver license office. DPS services are appointment-only; appointments can be scheduled up to 180 days in advance using the DPS appointment system:
- Appointments info page: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/driver-license-services-appointments
- Scheduler: https://www.txdpsscheduler.com/

---

DPS provides a “what to bring” checklist for first-time applications and recommends reviewing it before visiting an office. DPS also provides a step-by-step page for applying for a Texas driver license.
- Checklist PDF (What to bring when applying): https://www.dps.texas.gov/internetforms/Forms/DL-15.pdf
- Apply for a Texas Driver License: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/apply-texas-driver-license

## Q3. When is the best time to visit a driver license office?
If you are renewing, DPS recommends checking whether you can renew online to avoid an office visit. If you must visit in person (first-time applicants or not eligible online), services are appointment-only and can be scheduled up to 180 days in advance.
DPS notes full-time driver license offices are open Monday–Friday, with Mondays, Tuesdays, and Fridays often busiest, and busy seasons typically around spring break, summer, and after major holidays.
- Renew page: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/renew-your-texas-dl-cdl-motorcycle-license-or-id
- Appointments info page: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/driver-license-services-appointments
- Scheduler: https://www.txdpsscheduler.com/

## Q4. Can I renew my driver license or identification card online?
You may be eligible to renew online if you meet DPS criteria (including not renewing online last time). DPS directs customers to review the renewal page for eligibility.
- Renew page: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/renew-your-texas-dl-cdl-motorcycle-license-or-id

---

## Q5. What are the different ways you offer to renew a driver license or identification card?
DPS lists these options (within the eligible renewal window): Online, Telephone, Mail, or In-person at a driver license office.
- Renew page: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/renew-your-texas-dl-cdl-motorcycle-license-or-id

---

## Q6. Why am I unable to renew online even though my last transaction was done in a driver license office?
DPS explains that some office visits (such as address changes or duplicates) do not count as an “in-person renewal,” and that generally every other renewal must be completed in person. DPS points customers to online renewal eligibility requirements for full details.
- Renew page (eligibility requirements): https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/renew-your-texas-dl-cdl-motorcycle-license-or-id

## Q11. Why do I have to take a picture each time I conduct a driver license or identification card transaction in a driver license office?
DPS states the photograph is used as an authentication method to help prevent identification fraud and references Texas Transportation Code Section 521.059.

---

## Q12. Why am I required to present my social security card to obtain an identification card?
DPS states Texas Transportation Code Sections 521.101(d) and 521.142(g) require DPS to collect an applicant’s Social Security number for issuance of a driver license or ID card, and that this supports identity verification and fraud prevention.

---

## Q13. Do I have to go to the driver license office in the county that I live in?
No. DPS indicates you can handle DL/ID needs at any driver license office. Services are appointment-only and can be scheduled up to 180 days in advance:
- Appointments info page: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/driver-license-services-appointments
- Scheduler: https://www.txdpsscheduler.com/

DPS also notes that for replacement/renewal you may be eligible to complete the transaction online:
- TxT online services entry: https://txt.texas.gov/dps/driver-license-id-renewal-replacement

## Q14. How can I find out if I owe any fees or if I am suspended?
DPS directs customers to the Driver License Eligibility system to check eligibility and pay reinstatement fees:
- Driver License Eligibility (Texas.gov): https://txapps.texas.gov/txapp/txdps/dleligibility/login.do

---

## Q15. Do I have to provide proof of lawful presence for a change of address?
DPS states that if you are not a U.S. citizen, you must provide documentation proving lawful presence for any DL/ID transaction (including change of address).

---

## Q16. Why do I have to get a Commercial Learner Permit (CLP) prior to getting a Commercial Driver License (CDL)?
DPS explains that first-time CDL applicants (or those re-applying because their CDL has been expired more than two years) must obtain a CLP and hold it for at least two weeks under federal requirements. The CLP allows practice on public roadways with a qualified CDL holder in the cab.
DPS points to FMCSA for federal information and to DPS’s Texas CDL process page:
- FMCSA CDL overview: https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/registration/commercial-drivers-license/how-do-i-get-commercial-drivers-license
- Texas CDL process (DPS): https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/how-do-i-apply-commercial-driver-license

---

## Q17. I am out-of-state and my CDL expired, what do I do?
DPS states you must return to Texas and visit a driver license office, and recommends scheduling an appointment. DPS also notes CDL holders must undergo a national driver record status check and provide required self-certifications that can only be completed in a driver license office.
- Appointments info page: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/driver-license-services-appointments
- Scheduler: https://www.txdpsscheduler.com/
- Texas CDL process (DPS): https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/how-do-i-apply-commercial-driver-license

---

## Q18. Do I need a medical examiner’s certificate in order to apply for a Commercial Learner Permit (CLP)?
Yes. DPS states a valid medical certificate must be provided and maintained on file for all CLP and CDL holders. DPS directs customers to FMCSA to locate a certified medical examiner and to the DPS CDL page for Texas CDL details.
- FMCSA (certified medical examiner search): https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov
- Texas CDL process (DPS): https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/how-do-i-apply-commercial-driver-license

---

## Q19. How do I get a copy of my Texas birth certificate?
DPS directs customers to the Texas Department of State Health Services / Vital Statistics (and provides a phone number). For those born outside Texas, DPS advises contacting the home state’s vital statistics bureau.
