# Texas DPS — FAQ Section 12: Occupational License

## Q1. What documentation do I need to submit to get an occupational license?
DPS states you must send:
1) A certified copy of the petition  
2) A signed and stamped court order (must include allowable hours and counties)  
3) An SR-22 (proof of financial responsibility)  
4) A check or money order for all reinstatement fees owed  
5) The occupational license fee (write “CCR Code 0074” on the check or money order for the occupational license fee)

The documentation must include your full name, date of birth, and driver license number, and must be submitted to:

Texas Department of Public Safety  
ATTN: CCR  
PO BOX 15999  
Austin, TX 78761-5999  

Note: If you are not a U.S. citizen, you must submit proof of lawful presence.

Processing note: DPS advises allowing up to 21 business days for processing. The signed and stamped court order acts as a temporary driving permit for 45 days from the judge’s signature date, unless you have never had a driver license or your driver license has been expired for more than 2 years. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

## Q2. Can I pay my occupational license fee online?
Yes. DPS states the occupational license fee can be paid online through the License Eligibility page.

Alternatively, DPS states you may mail a check or money order for:
- $10 (1 year), or
- $20 (2 years)

Make it out to DPS, write “CCR Code 0074,” and mail to:

Texas Department of Public Safety  
ATTN: CCR  
PO BOX 15999  
Austin, TX 78761-5999 :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Q3. How long is my court order good for?
DPS states the court order is traditionally good for issuance of an occupational driver license for the period covering your suspension, in one-year increments. DPS notes cases vary and exceptions may apply. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## Q4. How soon can I drive once I get the court order?
DPS states the signed and stamped court order acts as a temporary driving permit for 45 days from the judge’s signature date, unless you have never had a driver license or your driver license has been expired for more than 2 years. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## Q5. Can I drive through another state with an occupational license?
No. DPS states the occupational license is only valid in the state of Texas. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## Q6. Why did I receive an occupational license with an interlock restriction on it?
DPS states that when someone receives an ALR suspension or is convicted of a DWI, the occupational license will be issued with an interlock restriction regardless of whether the court order includes an interlock requirement, in accordance with Code of Criminal Procedure, Subchapter A, Article 42A.407. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

---

## Q7. Will I have to retake my road exam in order to obtain an occupational license?
DPS states you will be required to retest if you have never had a Texas driver license or if your driver license has been expired for more than two (2) years. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

---

## Q8. Will I need to retake my road exam to get my driver license back once my occupational license has expired?
DPS states that if your driver license was expired for over two (2) years at the time the occupational license was issued and you did not take a road test prior to obtaining the occupational license, you will be required to retest. Otherwise, DPS states you can request a duplicate license without retesting. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
