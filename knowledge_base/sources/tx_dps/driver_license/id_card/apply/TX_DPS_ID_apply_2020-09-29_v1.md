# Texas DPS — How to Apply for a Texas Identification Card

Publisher: Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS)
Page date: 2020-09-29

## Service overview
- Texas DPS issues identification (ID) cards valid for up to six years to Texas residents.

## Proof requirements (to apply)
Applicant must provide proof of:
1) U.S. Citizenship or, if not a U.S. citizen, evidence of lawful presence
2) Texas residency
3) Identity
4) Social Security Number (SSN)

SSN verification note:
- SSN will be verified electronically with the U.S. government. If it cannot be verified, the ID card cannot be issued or renewed.

Helpful tools:
- REAL ID Document Check Application (interactive checklist builder).

## Application steps (in-person)
Once documents are gathered, DPS instructs applicants to:
1) Complete the identification card application before arriving (also available at offices).
2) Make an appointment at a driver license office.
3) Provide documentation to the license and permit specialist:
   - Application for issuance of an identification card
   - U.S. citizenship or evidence of lawful presence
   - Texas residency
   - Identity
4) Provide signature for DL/ID.
5) Provide thumbprints.
6) Have picture taken.
7) Pay the application fee.

## After the office visit (temporary card + delivery)
- After issuance, customer receives a temporary identification card.
- Before leaving, review printed information for accuracy; if incorrect, notify the specialist.
- New card arrives by mail in approximately 2–3 weeks.
- Mailing status can be checked on the DPS “mailing status” page.

## Special notes / special populations
### U.S. Military Veterans
- Some disabled veterans may qualify for a fee exemption on DL/ID. See Veteran Services page for eligibility.

### Temporary Visitors and Foreign Students
- Must meet additional requirements; see U.S. Citizenship or Lawful Presence Requirement page.

### Address Confidentiality Program
- DPS directs applicants to the Address Confidentiality Program page for information.

### Important eligibility restriction
- If you hold a driver license, you are no longer eligible to apply for an identification card unless you surrender your driver license at the time of application.

## Online option (Seniors age 65+)
Eligible Texans (65+, U.S. citizens, currently holding a REAL ID compliant Texas DL or CDL) may request to voluntarily surrender their license and apply for a Texas ID card without visiting a driver license office.
How to use:
1) Create or sign in to a Texas-by-Texas (TxT) account at Texas.gov.
2) Select the “Surrender DL for ID” option and submit required information to verify identity, citizenship, and age.
Status help:
- Customers may call 1-866-DL-RENEW (1-866-357-3639) to check status of the surrender request.
