## What this page is for
This page lists voluntary contribution opportunities and related program information available in Texas DPS driver license offices. Donations may also be offered online during driver license and identification card transactions.

Note: Contribution opportunities can only be made when conducting a driver license or identification card transaction and may not be available for all transactions.

---

## Programs and Contribution Opportunities (Summary)

### 1) Donate Life Texas (Organ, Eye, and Tissue Donation Registry)
- Donate Life Texas is the only official organ, eye, and tissue donor registry in Texas.
- Texans applying for or renewing a Texas driver license (DL), personal identification certificate (ID), or commercial driver license (CDL) may register as an organ donor.
- Important note: Registering as an organ donor through DPS covers donation after death only (not living donation).
- External reference: Donate Life Texas (official site): https://www.donatelifetexas.org

### 2) Living Organ Donation (Information)
- Living donation allows healthy individuals to donate a kidney or part of their liver while alive.
- Basic eligibility described: potential living donor must be 18+ and in good physical and mental health; screening is required.
- External references provided on the page include:
  - National Living Donor Assistance Center: https://www.livingdonorassistance.org
  - UNOS Group: https://unosgroup.org
  - The Living Bank: https://www.livingbank.org
  - LifeGift: https://www.lifegift.org
  - Texas DSHS (Living Organ Donation in Texas): https://www.dshs.texas.gov

### 3) Glenda Dawson Donate Life Texas Donor Registry (Funding information)
- Donate Life Texas is funded by donations, including those made during Texas DL/ID/CDL transactions and via online donation tools.
- Registry named in honor of Texas state representative Glenda Dawson.
- External reference (Texas DSHS): Glenda Dawson Donate Life Texas-Donor Registry: https://www.dshs.texas.gov

### 4) Blindness Education Screening and Treatment Program (BEST)
- BEST provides free vision screenings and financial assistance to Texas residents for medically urgent eye treatment with a referral from an ophthalmologist.
- Texans applying for or renewing a DL or ID may donate to BEST.
- External reference (Texas HHS): BEST program: https://www.hhs.texas.gov

### 5) Texas Veterans Assistance Fund
- Texans applying for or renewing a DL or ID may donate to this fund.
- Purpose: support veterans in need.
- Examples of assistance noted on the page:
  - Therapy
  - Addressing acute/chronic needs contributing to homelessness
  - Critical home repairs and accessibility improvements for service-connected disabled veterans
  - Rehabilitation support for justice-involved veterans via specialized courts
- External reference (Texas Veterans Commission): Support Texas Veterans: https://www.tvc.texas.gov
- Related DPS reference: Veteran Services: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/driver-license-and-identification-services-veterans

### 6) Survivors of Sexual Assault (Evidence Testing Support)
- House Bill 1729 (85th Texas Legislature) created a program for voluntary contributions during DL/ID/CDL original or renewal transactions.
- The program supports survivors of sexual assault or other sex offenses through the testing of collected evidence.
- Administered by the Criminal Justice Division of the Office of the Governor via a grant program distributing funds to qualifying labs/agencies.

### 7) Texas Bone Marrow Donation & Transplant
- House Bill 780 (87th Texas Legislature) created a bone marrow donor recruitment program.
- Purpose: education and recruitment regarding donor need, registration requirements, and medical guidelines.
- External reference (Texas DSHS): Bone Marrow Donation and Transplant program: https://www.dshs.texas.gov

### 8) Identification Fee Exemption Fund
- House Bill 123 (86th Texas Legislature) established the Identification Fee Exemption Fund.
- Purpose: assist with DL/ID fees for foster and homeless children/youths and victims/children of family or dating violence.
- Note: described as an account in the general revenue fund consisting of grants and donations made to the department.
