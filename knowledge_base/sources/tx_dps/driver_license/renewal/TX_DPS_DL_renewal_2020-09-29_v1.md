## 1) Renewal Window and Available Methods

Renewal window:
- You can renew up to 2 years before or after expiration.
- Exception: a CDL can only be renewed up to 1 year before expiration.

Renewal methods:
- Online
- By Telephone
- By Mail
- In-person at a driver license office

Special categories with additional requirements:
- DL/ID expired more than 2 years
- Senior drivers (79+)
- Provisional driver license (under 18)
- Limited-term driver license or ID card (temporary visitors)
- Currently living out-of-state

## 2) Online or Telephone Renewal (Eligibility + What You Need)

### 2.1 Driver License Renewal — Eligibility Requirements
To renew a Texas driver license online or by telephone, you must meet all applicable requirements:
- You renewed in person last time.
- You have a Class C, M, or CM driver license OR a CDL without a Hazmat endorsement.
  - Hazmat-endorsed licenses cannot be renewed online.
- Your license expires within 2 years OR has been expired for less than 2 years.
- You will be 18 within 30 days and hold a provisional license (special case).
- You are under 79 years of age.
- Your vision/physical/mental health has not significantly impacted your driving ability since last renewal.
- CDL only: you have a valid medical certificate on file.
- Your license is valid (not suspended/revoked/canceled/denied/disqualified) and you have no outstanding warrants/tickets.
  - License Eligibility system: https://txapps.texas.gov/txapp/txdps/dleligibility/login.do
- You are a U.S. citizen with a Social Security number on your record.

### 2.2 Identification (ID) Card Renewal — Eligibility Requirements
To renew a Texas ID card online or by telephone:
- You renewed your ID in person last time.
- Your ID expires within 2 years OR has been expired for less than 2 years.
- You are 18 years of age or older.
- You are a U.S. citizen with a Social Security number on your record.

### 2.3 What You Need to Complete an Online/Phone Renewal
If you meet eligibility requirements, you must have:
1) Your most recently issued driver license or ID card AND the audit number from the card.
2) A printer or an email address to receive your temporary driver license and payment receipt.
   - Note: These are not available when renewing by phone.
3) A valid credit card (MasterCard, Visa, Discover, or American Express).
4) The last four digits of your Social Security Number.

How to start renewal:
- Online renewal (Texas.gov / TxT): https://txt.texas.gov/dps/driver-license-id-renewal-replacement
- Telephone: 1-866-DL RENEW (1-866-357-3639)

Additional online feature:
- When renewing online, customers can provide and/or update emergency contact information.

## 3) Mail-in Renewal

- If you received an invitation to renew by mail, you may renew by mail.
- You can also choose online or phone renewal if you meet the eligibility requirements.
- To renew by mail, follow the instructions on the mailed form.

---

## 4) In-Person Renewal (When Not Eligible for Online/Phone/Mail)

If you are not eligible to renew online, by phone, or by mail, you must visit a driver license office.

Steps (DL / CDL / Motorcycle / ID):
1) Schedule an appointment:
   - Appointments info: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/driver-license-services-appointments
2) Complete the application:
   - Application (PDF): https://www.dps.texas.gov/internetforms/Forms/DL-14A.pdf
3) Bring required documentation:
   - Completed application
   - Proof of U.S. citizenship or lawful presence:
     https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/us-citizenship-or-lawful-presence-requirement
   - Proof of identity:
     https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/identification-requirements
4) Provide biometric information (signature and thumbprints).
5) Have your picture taken.
6) Pay the fee:
   https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/driver-license-fees
7) Pass the vision exam.

Provisional Driver License (under 18) — additional in-person requirement:
- Applicant must be accompanied by a parent or legal guardian.

## 5) Expired More Than Two Years (Important Rule)

- Driver licenses or ID cards expired for more than 2 years cannot be renewed.
- Guidance is provided on the “Apply” pages instead:
  - Apply for a Texas Driver License:
    https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/apply-texas-driver-license
  - Apply for a Texas Identification Card:
    https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/driver-license/how-apply-texas-identification-card
